📜 Preamble

Being aware of the need for a viable association that will protect the interests and welfare of all Cinematography and Broadcast students in the Rivers State University, determined to foster peaceful coexistence whilst exhibiting, realizing the need to promote and project the positive image of our association at the state, national and international levels, we hereby make, enact, and give unto ourselves this constitution.

This constitution is supreme and its provisions shall have binding force on all members of Cinematography and Broadcast Studies Students Association, Rivers State University.

Article 1 General Provisions

The general provisions of this constitution are for the purposes of simplifying certain terminologies and abbreviations used herein.

Section 1: Definitions

  1. All masculine words found in this constitution such as "he" and "his" are used for both genders.
  2. The term "association" used herein refers to CIBSSA.
  3. The term "body" or "bodies" is used in place of "committee" or "council" or "forum" in some contexts.
  4. The term "CRC" denotes CIBSSA Representative Council.
  5. The term "CEC" denotes CIBSSA Executive Council.
  6. The term "Aware" used in this constitution entails being politically inclined and conscious.
  7. The "allocation" used herein entails funds meant for personal usage as a form of salary for specific members for their services to the association, or association usage for the purposes of organizing programs, events and carrying out projects as stated in this constitution.
  8. The term "CDC" entails Constitution Drafting Committee.
  9. The "Class Leaders" stated herein are Class representatives from each department and level.
  10. The term "IEC" denotes the Independent Electoral Committee.
  11. The term "Comrade" is a title given to members of the association who holds or has held elective positions within the association or university.
  12. 'Referendum' means subjecting a particular question to direct vote of all the members of the association for decision/agreement.
  13. 'Law' means policies, provisions and regulations re-enacted by the CRC that are contained in or outside the constitution.
  14. Electoral committee: Means a body appointed to conduct all general elections and bye elections into the association.
  15. Emergency meeting(s): Means urgent meeting(s) or sitting(s) called in which urgent and important issues of the association are discussed.
  16. Extra ordinary meeting(s)/sitting(s): Means special meetings or sittings of the association that are not regular.
  17. Ordinary meeting(s)/sitting(s): Means normal/formal meeting(s) called in which issues of the association are discussed as stipulated in this constitution.
  18. Congress: Means a meeting of the representative of the student population called by the president, the HOD or any other officer acting on their behalf with their consent for the purpose of discussion, dialogue, dissemination of policy statements or directives as may be necessary from time to time.
  19. The term "Gross Misconduct" means a violation of the provisions of this constitution.

Section 2: Name of the Association

There shall exist an association known as Cinematography and Broadcast Studies Student's Association (CIBSSA).

Section 3: Motto of the Association

The motto of CIBSSA shall be "Beyond Telling, We Show"

Section 4: Principles of the Association

CIBSSA shall be founded on the principles of honesty, dependability, innovation, optimism, modesty, self-discipline, cooperation, non-violence, selfless service, whilst also putting its students' welfare at the heart of its affairs.

Section 5: Aims and Objectives of the Association

  1. To empower young people of all background through its innovative training programme, enabling its learners to develop and contribute to the economic regeneration of its area.
  2. To exercise the basic constitutional rights such as freedom of expression, access to information and equity amongst its members.
  3. To cultivate a leadership chain, based on rational, genuine and sincere love for fellow students and noble aspirations for the collective good of CIBSSA.
  4. To improve and promote the educational status of members of the association by means of lectures, seminars and publications with due reference to their general rights and obligations under law.
  5. To affiliate with other associations with similar aims and objectives.
  6. To imbue and encourage the sense of responsibility and dedication to the cause of CIBSSA.

Article 2 Membership and Privileges

Section 1: Ordinary Membership

A. Ordinary Membership:

  1. Ordinary membership shall be automatically given to all duly registered students of the department of Cinematography and Broadcast Studies, RSU.
  2. All undergraduate students are eligible to be members provided that they have paid their association dues.

B. Honorary Membership:

  1. Persons of reasonable standing and attainment may be appointed honorary members by The Student Executive Council (CEC) subject to ratification by Students' Representative Council (CRC).
  2. After such persons are deemed fit by reason of their interest and contributions to students development, welfare and academic matters, especially as relates to the association and the university. The appointment of honorary members shall be ratified by a two third majority of members present and voting at a properly constituted session of the CRC.
  3. There shall not be more than five honorary members approved in the lifespan of any association's tenure (administrative year).

Section 2: Privileges and Rights of Members

  1. CIBSSA shall protect the interests of its members so long they are acting within the contents of this constitution.
  2. Members are entitled to fully observe their rights to freedom of speech and fair hearing during trials as well as during congress.
  3. Members shall have the right to participate in elections, to vote and be voted for, as well as any other fundamental rights that is due them.
  4. Members shall deal with bodies or associations who are affiliated with CIBSSA through the Executive Council.
  5. Ordinary members shall have the privilege to be appointed as members of any committee to be set up by the association.

Section 3: Duties and Obligations of Members

  1. All members shall remain conscious and uphold the principles of CIBSSA and public trust placed in them.
  2. All members shall pay faculty levies or other fees for the smooth running of CIBSSA when the need arises.
  3. All members shall give their best when called upon to serve in any committee, upon approval by the executive council.
  4. All members are accountable for their decisions and action to the public and to CIBSSA.
  5. Members shall respect fellow members, promote and support this principle by leadership and example.
  6. All members shall pledge their loyalty to the association by discharging their assigned duties diligently and effectively, either as an individual or committee.

Section 4: Obligations of CIBSSA to Members

  1. CIBSSA shall be obliged to always protect the interests of members working in line with the aims, objectives and principles of the association, geared towards the positive developments and growth of CIBSSA.
  2. CIBSSA shall always advise and encourage members to pursue excellence in order to be useful to the university and society at large.
  3. CIBSSA shall organize yearly programmes where members who have distinguished themselves by serving in various capacities in the association shall be acknowledged and given honors or awards.

Article 3 Constituent Bodies / Structure of the Association

Section 1: Organs of CIBSSA

There shall be three organs of the association to ensure proper check and balance of government within the association, namely:

  • The CIBSSA Executive Council
  • The CIBSSA Representative Council
  • The Congress

Section 2: The CIBSSA Executive Council (CEC)

There shall be an executive council of the association hereinafter referred to as CEC. It shall comprise of:

  1. The President
  2. The Vice-President
  3. The Secretary – General
  4. The Assistant Secretary-General
  5. The Financial Secretary
  6. The Public Relations Officer
  7. The Director of Social
  8. The Welfare Secretary
  9. The Director of Sports
  10. The Auditor General

Section 3: The Representative Council

  1. There shall be a departmental representative council of the association, hereinafter referred to as CRC.
  2. Which shall comprise student representatives stipulated in this constitution in such a manner that the students shall represent a set of students in the same academic department of the university, hereinafter referred to as the constituency.
  3. The CRC shall consist of members from all levels in the department.
  4. The CRC shall consist of minimum of twelve (12) members.
  5. Each level shall be represented by at least three (3) members.
  6. The Speaker and Deputy speaker shall be in their penultimate level.
  7. In the absence of candidates for the position of Speaker or Deputy speaker from the penultimate year/level, then such position(s) shall be open to other levels except first year/100 level.
  8. The Deputy Clerk and Chief Whip/Sergeant at arms; preferably but not limited to first year/100 level students of the department.

Officers of the CRC:

  1. Speaker
  2. Deputy Speaker
  3. Clerk
  4. Leader of the House
  5. Deputy Clerk
  6. Chief Whip/Sergeant at Arms
  1. The members of the CRC alone as contained in this constitution shall elect these officers from among themselves at least two weeks before their inauguration.
  2. The CRC in-house election shall be conducted and supervised by the Staff adviser or any lecturer of the department.

Section 4: The Congress

  1. There shall be congress at least two times a semester and it shall serve as a means of disseminating information in the Department; the secretary general shall call for congress, after due consultation with the president and executive council.
  2. The congress shall consist of all duly registered CIBSSA students and the HOD of the Department or his representative and one or more lecturers of the department.
  3. The President shall preside over every congress meeting and as such, delegate such responsibilities to his Vice if he is unavailable.
  4. The quorum for decision making in the congress is by a two-thirds majority present and voting.
  5. Decision taken by simple majority of members present at congress shall be binding on all members of CIBSSA.
  6. The executive council shall call for an emergency congress after due consultation with the representative council or at the request of a simple majority of a sitting congress.
  7. The congress shall consist of all the executives, advisory board members, appointees, committees and ordinary members of the association with each level being effectively represented by their class representatives and others as the case may be.
  8. Shall have power to elect officers into the different arms of the association, in the case where the electoral guidelines permit such.
  9. Shall have power to elect officers to fill vacancies in the case of a by-election as provided in this constitution.
  10. The congress shall have the power to take decisions on matters which have been established by the CEC and CRC to be beyond their powers, and shall take decisions on any matter placed before it. Such decisions shall be subject to the approval of the HOD.

Section 5: Conduct During Congress

  1. All members of the association are to conduct themselves in an orderly manner during congress and meetings.
  2. Members shall signify by raising their hands if they have any contributions to make during congress or meetings and they shall be recognized by the president before speaking.
  3. All members of the association shall stand upright before addressing the congress.
  4. All executives, appointees and committee members of the association are to be in attendance for all congresses, and if for any reason they are to be absent, information must be sent to the president at least six hours before congress and the house shall be informed at the start of congress about their absence.
  5. Executive members who are absent for two congress sittings without any understandable and important reasons, shall pay a fine of two thousand naira only, while class representatives or class leaders are to be fined one thousand naira only. All fines are to be collected by the provost and deposited accordingly.

Article 4 Functions and Meetings

Section 1: Of the CIBSSA Executive Council (CEC)

  1. The CEC shall represent the views of the association on any matter affecting the association after due resolution has been passed by the CRC or the Congress.
  2. Shall have powers to hold discussions with the management of the department on matters affecting the students as well as the association in conjunction with the Speaker or, in his/her absence, the Deputy Speaker.
  3. Shall perform other functions as may be provided by the Constitution and resolutions of the CRC.

Section 2: Meetings of the CIBSSA Executive Council (CEC)

  1. The CIBSSA Executive Council shall meet at least once a month and as many times as necessary in its emergency sessions.
  2. It shall be the duty of the President acting through or with the Secretary General, to summon meetings of the Executive Council.
  3. Notice of the Executive Council meetings containing the agenda must be communicated through writing by the Secretary General to all members of the Council at least 12 hours before the meeting.
  4. Eight members of CEC shall constitute a quorum at any of its meetings with the President or Vice President, Secretary General, or the Assistant Secretary General, The Financial Secretary or Auditor General in attendance.
  5. Decisions in the Executive Council meeting shall be arrived at by two-thirds majority of the members present and voting.
  6. The Executive Council, may decide the manner in which it shall vote on any matter before it.

Section 3: Functions of the CIBSSA Representative Council (CRC)

  1. This body shall serve as the legislative arm of the association and it shall be tasked with making laws consistent with the faculty, school and society while also check-mating the activities and excesses of the executives and members of the association.
  2. The CIBSSA Representative Council shall conduct an in-house election two weeks after the executive council are sworn in to produce its officers.
  3. It shall approve sessional budget and supplementary budgetary allocations presented to it by the executive council through the president when necessary and shall upon the request of the executive council, approve an amount for the executive to work with, pending the final approval of the association's session budget.
  4. The CRC shall investigate matters concerning cases of misappropriation, diversion of association funds and other financial frauds, misconduct and usurp of power within the association. A review of such investigation shall be sent to the HOD in written publication.
  5. Shall have power to summon any member of the executive council or officers of the association for questioning and also approve payment to officers of the association and agents.
  6. Shall suspend or stop payment or use of money from the association's accounts or any financial transaction in the name of the association when compelled by situations provided such decision have been approved by the HOD of the Department.
  7. This body shall draw up guidelines, rules and standing orders to guide its proceedings and day to day running and shall perform oversight functions.
  8. Members of the CRC shall constitute at least 70% of the constitution drafting or amendment committee.

Section 4: The Representative Council Meetings

  1. There shall be CRC meetings at least once a month according to a duly published calendar of such meetings, with the exception of emergency meetings.
  2. CRC meetings shall be summoned by the Speaker but in his absence his Deputy shall summon such meetings with his consent unless otherwise provided in this constitution.
  3. Notice of such meetings must be communicated to the members in writing by the clerk of the House at least forty-eight hours to the day of the meeting; in the case of emergency sitting a twelve hours notice shall suffice.
  4. Quorum of the CRC shall be two-thirds of its members with the Speaker or Deputy Speaker, Clerk or Deputy Clerk and Chief Whip in attendance.
  5. Decisions in the CRC shall be by simple majority of the votes of those present. In the event of a tie, the speaker shall have a casting vote and voting shall be by show of hands unless otherwise decided by the CRC.
  6. They shall meet at request in writing of 2/3 of its membership.
  7. The CRC shall have the power to excommunicate any member found wanting from the association. This is subject to the final decision of the president.
  8. The CRC shall have a mace and a gavel that shall be the symbols of its authority and binding force on its resolutions.
  9. The gavel shall be placed in front of the Speaker or his Deputy in the absence of the Speaker, which shall be hit when the house reaches a decision on any matter.

Section 5: The Mace

  1. The CRC shall have a mace that shall be the symbol of authority.
  2. The mace shall be conspicuously placed on the table in front of the speaker (or the deputy speaker in the absence of the speaker) who shall preside over the activities of the house at all sittings except at the committee session when the mace shall be lowered.
  3. The speaker shall take a lower seat at any committee session of the house with the mace lowered throughout the period. Then the speaker shall be addressed as "Mr. Chairman Sir".
  4. Only the chief whip, who is recognized also as the sergeant at arms of the house, shall bear or handle the mace which shall be in his/her custody and it shall be produced at every sitting.

Article 5 Duties of the Officers of the Association

Section 1: The President

  1. The President shall be the head of the Executive Council and chairman of the congress and all meetings of the executive council.
  2. He shall be the leader of the official representatives of the association to the authorities, the school management and any other body.
  3. He shall authenticate all receipts of payments or expenditures by CIBSSA, and he shall give annual reports on accounts and stewardship to the congress at the end of his tenure.
  4. He shall have the exclusive power (by veto of his office) to adjourn motions, meetings or congress when and where peace is deemed threatened, provided such motions are not for his impeachment.
  5. He shall be a principal signatory to all cheques issued for payments or withdrawals by CIBSSA.
  6. He shall endorse all documents relating to the affairs of the executive council.
  7. He shall have an official stamp alongside his signature to authenticate all documents, journals and letters of the association.
  8. He shall have casting vote during election and any other decisions requiring voting.
  9. He shall be a principal signatory to all letters, articles and memoranda.
  10. He shall coordinate the activities of other offices of the executive council.
  11. He shall appoint aides or assistants and also setup committees to enable his administration function to the best interests of the association and its members. And other functions as stated in this constitution therein.

Section 2: Vice President

  1. The Vice President shall carry out all the functions of the president, where the president is unable to discharge those functions as provided in this constitution.
  2. He shall carry out any other functions as may be assigned to him by the president, as such other functions are not illegal or inconsistent with the provisions of this constitution.
  3. He shall head committees' setup by the executive council for easy facilitation of policies and programmes.
  4. Shall succeed the President if the President absconds, abdicates, dies, resigns, or is incapacitated by reason of frustration, insanity, suspension, impeachment, and/or expulsion.

Section 3: Secretary General

  1. The Secretary General shall be in charge of the association's administration, which includes the arrangement of meetings of the executive council under the directive of the president and convey same to the other executives, drafting all administrative letters, articles and memoranda of the association, dissemination of information to all other member bodies of the association as circumstances may necessitate the services of those other bodies.
  2. He shall be one of the official signatories of all letters, articles and memoranda of CIBSSA.
  3. He shall take minutes at the executive meetings and congress and will read same in the next meeting day or congress sitting and convey the decisions or outcome of all executive meetings to the congress.
  4. He shall make an annual report of all the activities of CIBSSA during his time in office and convey same to the congress after due consideration by the executive members.

Section 4: Assistant Secretary General

  1. Shall act on behalf of the Secretary General in his/her absence and upon the approval of the Secretary General, shall assist him or her in accordance with the provisions of this constitution.
  2. Shall be responsible for the distribution of stationeries to the various arms of the association and keep records of such distributions.
  3. Shall be responsible for the counting and recording of votes at all meeting(s) of the CEC.
  4. Shall perform any other function as may be duly assigned to him/her by the Secretary General.
  5. Shall replace the Secretary General if the Secretary General absconds, abdicates, resigns, is incapacitated by reason of frustration, insanity, dead, impeached, suspended and or expelled.

Section 5: The Financial Secretary

  1. The Financial Secretary shall be responsible for the collection of all monies, levies and donations payable to CIBSSA.
  2. He shall accurately keep record of all financial transactions entered into by CIBSSA, issue appropriate receipts for all payments, and shall deposit all payments into the association's account within seventy-two hours of collection and issuance of receipts.
  3. He shall be signatory to all cheques issued about payments into and withdrawals from CIBSSA account.
  4. He shall present an annual statement of accounts of the association's financial transactions each year, to the Auditor General as well as the congress.
  5. He shall ensure that funds disbursed to officers are judiciously accounted for before such officers are allowed more withdrawals.

Section 6: Public Relations Officer

  1. Shall sign any association's publication aired or printed in the mass media.
  2. Shall be responsible for all association's publications, periodicals etc.
  3. Shall maintain the Association's Notice Boards and suggestion boxes.
  4. Shall be Chairman of the Editorial Board of the association and ensure compliance with the relevant sections of the Students Handbook.
  5. Shall plan and maintain good relationship with external bodies.
  6. Shall at all times keep the students informed through approved means with substantial information on contemporary issues.
  7. Shall be in custody of the Association's public-address system.
  8. Shall carry out any other function(s) that may be assigned to him/her from time to time and in accordance with this Constitution.

Section 7: The Director of Socials

  1. The Director of Socials shall plan and organize recreational activities such as seminars, lectures, outdoor events and in-house social activities.
  2. He shall be the chairman of the social committee which shall be responsible for the planning, overseeing and success of the association's events and public activities.
  3. He shall be responsible for the provision and upkeep of CIBSSA facilities or equipments.
  4. He shall work with the Public Relations Officer in matters of social outreach for CIBSSA.
  5. Shall ensure that clubs and organizations not affiliated to the association are denied the use of the association's facilities.
  6. Shall submit all proceeds to Financial Secretary within 48 hours of the end of such activities.
  7. Shall carry out other functions as may be provided by this constitution.

Section 8: The Director of Welfare

  1. The Director of Welfare shall ensure the students welfare is always at the center of the association's activities and administration.
  2. He shall relate with the students or members of the association on behalf of the executive council in assisting the members financially or otherwise as the case may be.
  3. He shall be chairman of the welfare committee and he shall ensure the rights of all members of the association are protected accordingly.
  4. He shall provide refreshments during congress and executive meetings.

Section 9: The Director of Sports

  1. The Director of Sports shall be in charge of the association's sporting activities.
  2. He shall be the chairman of the association's sports committee and together with his committee members, he shall ensure the smooth blending of sports and academic activities within the university.
  3. He shall provide sporting equipments or facilities and encourage members of the association to participate in sporting activities.
  4. Shall co-ordinate all sporting activities of the association.
  5. Shall carry out other functions as may be provided by this constitution.

Section 10: The Auditor General

  1. The Auditor General shall have unlimited access to the financial records, documents and accounts of CIBSSA so as to enable him audit the association's accounts and present his reports to the congress at the end of every semester.
  2. He shall exercise his powers in the discharge of his duties, only in accordance with the provisions of this constitution.
  3. He shall advise against reckless spending in the association and convey such displeasure in congress, as the case may be.
  4. Report such matters to the congress and enforce appropriate punishments or fines as stated in this constitution.
  5. He shall diplomatically coordinate visitors who may want to have an audience with any member of the association during congress.
  6. Shall in discharge of his/her functions have access to all books and accounts of the association in the custody of any association's official or agents.
  7. Shall prepare an audited statement of account from the association bankers and present the same to the CEC and head of CRC at least once every semester.
  8. Shall audit the accounts of the association in general, and the different arms in particular.
  9. He shall also carry out all duties assigned to the provost or more, as may be required of him by the executive council or congress.

Section 11: The Speaker of the Legislative Arm

  1. Shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected as Speaker by the CRC from among its members.
  3. Shall not preside over meeting(s)/sitting(s) if there is an impeachment motion against him or her.
  4. Shall preside at/conduct meetings of the CRC.
  5. May walk out, or place on probation from the Council, any person or group of persons who in his/her view and opinion disturbs any session of the CRC meeting(s)/sittings/plenary session(s) for a period not exceeding one month or two ordinary sittings provided such action is endorsed by two-thirds of the members of the CRC at the sitting.
  6. Shall if in his/her opinion found out that the provision stipulated in the Association's Constitution is not enough to deal with such person(s) as above, refer such persons to the Staff Adviser or the department disciplinary committee (if any) or to the Students Judicial Council through the Honourable Justice of the faculty for more serious penalty.
  7. Shall have a winning vote in the event of a tie otherwise he is precluded from voting at meetings of the CRC.
  8. Shall summon meetings of the CRC through the Clerk of the House.
  9. Shall carry out all such function(s) as are assigned to him under other articles of the Constitution.
  10. Shall be in-charge of the parliament.

Section 12: Deputy Speaker

  1. Shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected by the house from among its members.
  3. Shall act on behalf of the Speaker in the absence of the former and shall assist him or her in the performance of his/her function.
  4. Shall assume the office of the Speaker in the event of insanity, frustration, or any such debilitating acts of nature, impeachment, death or resignation, but shall relinquish this post as soon as the post is filled by a by-election.
  5. Shall be removed from office in the same manner as the Speaker.
  6. Shall not be entitled to vote and shall be the Electoral officer of the CRC.
  7. Shall carry out all such other function(s) as are assigned to him or her by other articles of this Constitution.

Section 13: Clerk of the House

  1. Shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected by the house from among its members and shall be referred to as the "CLERK OF THE HOUSE".
  3. Shall convene meetings of the CRC on the directives of the Speaker of the CRC and ensure that notice(s) of such meeting(s) containing the agenda of such meeting reach the members at least 48 hours to the said meeting(s)/sitting(s).
  4. Shall record and keep in good custody, copies of the proceedings of CRC meetings.
  5. Shall arrange for CRC sitting(s)/meeting(s) venue.
  6. Shall sign all resolutions of the House with the Speaker.
  7. Shall perform all such other function(s) as are assigned to him/her under this Constitution.
  8. Shall prepare the agenda for any sitting in consultation with the Speaker.
  9. Shall purchase and make available, stationeries for the CRC.

Section 14: The Leader of the House

  1. Shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected by the house from among its members and shall be referred to as "LEADER OF THE HOUSE".
  3. Shall lead the debate at the floor of the house.
  4. Shall schedule legislations for consideration.
  5. Shall assist the Speaker with:
    1. Program development
    2. Policy formation
    3. Policy decision
  6. Shall coordinate committee activities to ensure a steady flow of bills for floor debate.

Section 15: The Deputy Clerk

  1. Shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected by the House from among its members.
  3. Shall act on behalf of the Clerk of the House in the absence of the former and shall assist him or her in the performance of his/her functions.
  4. Carry out all such other function(s) as assigned to him or her by the Clerk of the House and/or Speaker.
  5. Shall replace the Clerk if the Clerk is impeached, dies, resigns, absconds, abdicates, is suspended or incapacitated, etc.

Section 16: Chief Whip

  1. The Chief Whip shall be an elected member of the CRC.
  2. Shall be elected by the house from among its members.
  3. Shall bear the mace.
  4. Shall ensure orderliness in the House.
  5. Shall be responsible for control and admission of visitors to the CRC galleries.
  6. Shall usher in the Speaker in all meetings conducted or presided over by him.
  7. He shall count the votes as directed and supervised by the Deputy Speaker.
  8. Shall be in charge of entertainment at the sittings of the CRC.
  9. Shall carry out any other functions as may be assigned to him or her by the Speaker and this Constitution.

Article 6 Order of Precedence

The order of precedence shall be as follows:

  1. President
  2. Vice President
  3. Speaker
  4. Secretary General
  5. Deputy Speaker
  6. Other members of the CEC
  7. Other members of CRC

Article 7 Advisory Committee, Staff Adviser and Patrons/Matrons

There shall be advisory committee/board, staff adviser, patrons and matrons in CIBSSA all of which shall have distinctive roles to play in the association.

Section 1: The Advisory Committee/Board

  1. There shall be an advisory committee for CIBSSA which comprises of all conscious emeritus officials from CEC, CRC, emeritus Class leaders and SUG, from each department ranging from students in their second year, third year and final year.
  2. The advisory committee shall have a chairman, secretary, public relations officer and seven or more members from students in their second year, third year and final year who met the criteria.
  3. The chairman of the aforementioned body shall be an outstanding student or member of the association in his third year or final year with great personality, one who had previously served in various capacities within or outside the association.
  4. The Advisory board shall play an advisory role to the association's leadership and shall always protect the interests of all CIBSSA students.

Section 2: Staff Adviser

  1. The staff adviser shall be a lecturer from the department known for supporting student unionism and the association.
  2. He shall be nominated by the executive council, to the HOD of the Department and upon his approval, such lecturer shall be staff adviser to the association for three years.
  3. The staff adviser is to serve as a bridge between the association's leadership and management of the department.
  4. He shall advise and guide the association's leadership.

Section 3: Patrons/Matrons

  1. There shall be a patron or matron for CIBSSA to serve as a father or mother figure to the association.
  2. The HOD Department of Cinematography and Broadcast Studies shall be the official GRAND patron or matron of CIBSSA.

Article 8 The Constitution and Election

There shall be a constitution for the students of Cinematography And Broadcast Studies in the Rivers State University to guide its students and there must be elections to produce officers of the association.

Section 1: The Constitution Drafting Committee

  1. There shall be a constitution drafting committee for CIBSSA herein after referred to as the CDC.
  2. A five- or seven-man committee shall be tasked with drafting, enacting and amending the constitution as provided.
  3. This committee shall consist of the chairman who shall be a third year or final year student of outstanding character and enthusiastic about the association, along with a secretary and public relations officer who are in their second year or third year and at least a member who is in his first year. All of which shall share the same values as their chairman.
  4. The president shall appoint and inaugurate the CDC in congress. Membership must be drawn from at least 70% of the CRC.
  5. The committee shall be given a fixed time to produce the constitution and upon completion of this task, they shall be disbanded.

Section 2: Drafting and Amending the Constitution

  1. A constitution shall be drafted for CIBSSA if there be non-available by the CDC, appointed by the president.
  2. The constitution shall have binding force on all members of the association irrespective of status or position.
  3. This constitution shall be liable for review or amendment five years from the day of its adoption to suit modern day realities in the association and society.
  4. The constitution shall serve as a guide, reference and primary law to members of the association if there be conflict of interests or in situations which may seem complicated or pose a threat to positive development and growth of CIBSSA.
  5. The constitution shall pass its first and second reading in congress, then it shall be sent to the HOD for his approval before the association shall adopt it as its primary law.

Section 3: Formation of Electoral Committee

  1. There shall be an electoral body for CIBSSA called the Independent Electoral Committee.
  2. The aforementioned body shall be charged with the responsibility of organizing, controlling and ensuring the free, fair and credible processes of all electoral activities of the association.
  3. This committee shall consist of 7 duly registered students of the Department or association.
  4. The chairman of this committee shall be a student of outstanding character and exemplary personality with history of honesty and good conduct.
  5. The other members of the committee are to be nominated from each level in the department and they must possess the same values as their chairman.

Section 4: Positions and Requirements for Election

  1. There shall be an election for offices in the executive council and representative council respectively.
  2. Year one students shall be eligible to contest for the position of Provost 2 only in the executive council.
  3. Students vying for executive offices such as that of the president, vice president and secretary general must have a minimum GPA or CGPA of 3.50, while students vying for other executive offices must have a minimum GPA or CGPA of 3.00.
  4. Students vying for offices in the representative council must have a minimum GPA or CGPA of 2.80, while the leader of the house and chief whip shall be appointed by the president and the other members of the council shall be nominated from their constituencies.

Section 5: Election Proper

  1. The outgoing president of the association shall allocate funds to the IEC to enable them make all necessary provisions and preparations for the election and the independent electoral committee shall conclude all electoral preparations and processes under three weeks of formation.
  2. There shall be observers ranging from high profile students of the association to high profile students of the university and lecturers from the faculty to ensure the electoral process is credible as stated in this constitution.
  3. There shall be a by-election in situations where two or more positions in the association are vacant and such election shall be conducted in congress.
  4. Upon the conclusion of a successful electoral process, there shall be an inauguration organized by the IEC two weeks after the election to usher in the new officers of the association and also hand them their certificate of return.

Article 9 Finance, Budgets and Projects

The following bodies shall have financial support from the department and association.

Section 1: The Executive Council

  1. The executive council shall be entitled to 70% of the association's allocation to enable them carry out programs, events and running of their administration.
  2. There shall be a record of all funds allocated to the executives and the manner in which it was used, prepared and signed by the financial secretary and auditor of the association.
  3. In situations where CIBSSA is short on funds or allocation to carry out necessary projects, the executives shall source out funds from members of the association to enable them carry out or complete such projects.
  4. The executive council acting through the financial secretary shall receive sessional payments of the association's dues from CIBSSA students and record such payments.

Section 2: The CIBSSA Representative Council

  1. The representative council shall have 20% of the association's allocations so as to enable them carry out research, make laws and function in their full capacity as officers of the association.
  2. Prior to the above clause, the CRC shall be paid allowances according to the number of sittings/meetings and not exceeding the speculated percentage.

Section 3: The Advisory Committee/Board

  1. The association's advisory committee as elder statesmen shall receive 10% of the association's allocation during their time in office for their roles in the upbringing of more conscious members of the association and also protecting the association's interests.

Article 10 Appointments, Committees and Class Leaders

Section 1: Appointments

  1. The president, as first member of the association and chairman of the executive council shall make all appointments and convey such appointments to the representative council for approval before making such appointments public or before congress.
  2. All executive members of the association shall make only one appointment, either for a personal assistant or a special assistant, excluding the president as provided in this constitution.
  3. The president shall make the following necessary appointments for the smooth running of his administration and discharge of duties:
    • Chief of Staff
    • Chief Press Secretary
    • Personal Assistant
    • Special Adviser to The President
    • Any other appointment he wishes to make during his time in office

Section 2: Committees

There shall be the following committees for CIBSSA:

  1. Welfare Committee — shall assist the director of welfare in discharging his duties as stated earlier in this constitution.
  2. The Electoral Committee — shall be responsible for organizing and overseeing the electoral processes of the association.
  3. The Constitution Drafting Committee — shall be charged with drafting or amending the CIBSSA Constitution.
  4. The Social Committee — shall be tasked with organizing the CIBSSA week amongst other events.
  5. The Sports Committee — shall be in charge of promoting sporting activities and organizing sporting activities in the association.
  6. The Editorial/Publicity Committee — shall be in charge of the CIBSSA magazine publication, amongst other publications.
  7. Any other committee which the president wishes to form, for the positive developments and growth of the association, pending approval from the representative council.

Members shall function to the best interests of the association when called upon by the president to be a part of the aforementioned committees and they shall respond to such call with diligence and enthusiasm.

Section 3: Class Leaders

  1. There shall be class leaders cut across all class levels.
  2. The Department class representative shall be the head of the class leadership, while his assistant shall be his deputy. In the absence of both persons, the most influential or conscious class member shall step up to head the class leadership.
  3. The Department president and his executives shall conduct the department class representative's election for all class levels under three weeks of resumption for every academic session. The president, as the case may be, may opt to appoint any student as the Rep.
  4. The class representatives shall send a list of all class leaders to the president of the association and further introduce them to the executive council and representative council respectively in a joint meeting.
  5. Department Class Rep can hold duo positions concurrently.

Article 11 Inauguration, Academic and Political Activities

There shall be an inauguration for members of the executive council, the representative council, appointees, and committee members as well as provisions to further improve academic and political activities in the department.

Section 1: Inauguration

  1. Upon the conclusion of a successful electoral process, there shall be an inauguration organized by the IEC under a period of two weeks after the election to usher in the new officers of the association and also hand them their certificate of return.
  2. The executive council, representative council, appointees and committees shall officially start discharging their duties to the association within twenty-four hours of inauguration.

Section 2: Academic Activities

  1. The president alongside his executive council shall kickstart the academic activities for the new session by conducting the class representative's election for all class levels within two weeks of school resumption.
  2. The executive council and representative council shall work towards ensuring a conducive academic environment for CIBSSA students.
  3. The executive council and representative council shall liaise with the lecturers of the department to organize and ensure lectures go on unhindered.

Section 3: Political Activities

  1. Sequel to the drafting of this constitution, the political consciousness of the association was at an all-time low, and as such the constitution drafting committee thought it wise to make provisions for political encouragement within the association; the executives shall reignite the consciousness of members of the association from time to time.

Article 12 Impeachment, Resignation and Suspension

Section 1: Impeachment

Provisions for the impeachment of officers of the association shall be in accordance with the procedures and guidelines established by this constitution and the CRC.

Section 2: Resignation

Any officer of the association who wishes to resign from their position shall do so in writing, addressed to the appropriate authority as provided in this constitution.
